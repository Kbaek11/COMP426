Facing an elimination game in the first round of the 1989 Eastern Conference playoff series between the Chicago Bulls and the Cleveland Cavaliers, Michael Jordan hits the game winner to clinch the series at 3-2. The buzzer beater hit by Michael Jordan with three seconds left became known as "The Shot", becoming an iconic moment in sports history. "The Shot" symbolized the beginning of the Chicago Bulls dynasty led by Michael Jordan.
